TL;DR: its a good modern game, but a bad remake. If you dont care about the original that much, youll have a great time. When Capcom announced Resident Evil 4 Remake, I thought to myself that if they transfer the combat mechanics from Remake 2 and Remake 3, i.e. headshots not having 100 stagger chance, clumsier movement, imprecise aiming, and so on, theyre going to ruin it. All of these aspects work for a survival horror, a slower type of game. Original Resi 4 is an action/arcade game which boasts predictability, crisp and precise movement and aiming. I wanted to give RE4R a chance. I lowered all my expectations and opened my mind. Surprisingly, for the first few chapters, I got sucked into the game. I grew to like parrying, even though it can be unreliable at times. Unfortunately, as the time went on, chapters passed, a feeling started to creep in. A question. Am I enjoying RE4R on its own merits, or because its a remake of Resident Evil 4?... ...and so began the downwards spiral. I started noticing changes to encounters and mechanics not because they would improve the game, but for the sake of it: The mentioned headshots having 100 stagger chance? Nerfed. Now headshotting is very unreliable, especially at higher difficulties. Knife is too powerful? Now it has durability which is so low that upgrading it is almost a must. Even then, youll need to pick up temporary knives so as not to go bankrupt from constant repairs. Whats worse, in RE4R its your primary defensive tool. If you have no knives left, youre sht out of luck. No wonder people rush to unlock Primal Knife, a bonus weapon which can be upgraded to have infinite durability. Tactical Vest reduces damage too much? Now it can straight up completely block some damage, but is made of tissue paper and breaks after a few harder hits. Of course, repairing it very expensive. Its good only for boss fights and Professional mode. Game too easy after people learned to aim well? Now instead of creeping up on you, enemies straight up rush you, and their grabs have insane tracking. Being hit by a heavy attack does not grant you any i-frames, so you can be stunlocked to death. Leon is slow as are all his reloads. Parrying is required for defense, but Leon needs a few frames to be able to parry at all, so if you start aiming, or reloading, or changing your weapon, you cannot parry, especially at close range. This is very noticeable on Professional. At least they removed QTEs, right? Not really, they reduced them, but lets be honest: parrying is a sort of QTE. Evading literally is one, as a prompt is displayed on the screen. But oh, they added stealth! Except it works exactly like in The Evil Within, which was criticized for the simplistic, low effort way it was implemented. Back in 2014. There are some good changes, but most of them are tied to atmosphere and character writing. Completely unnecessary remake. A product of remake culture, purely for the money. I didnt even bother playing Separate Ways DLC. It can be fun, since its Resident Evil 4 at heart. Good game, but a bad remake. Buy only on sale.

Worth every penny. Resident Evil 4 Remake is a masterclass in how to modernize a game while still maintaining the core of what made the original so special. The OG RE4 is one of my favorite games of all time, and I was originally skeptical of the remake, but Capcom managed to keep everything that made the 2005 classic a timeless masterpiece while adding to the game and making smart changes to certain parts. They managed to retain the intensity and atmosphere of the original, and elevated them thanks to modern graphics and improved gameplay mechanics. In terms of gameplay, everything you know and love from the OG is present in the remake in its full glory, with the added mechanics of stealth and the knife parry adding new ways to approach combat encounters (The parry mechanic is by far my favorite thing added in the remake). Since the tank controls were removed in place of traditional third person movement, it allows for greater mobility in combat. To compensate for improved mobility, the enemy AI has been revamped to make the ganados more aggressive during combat and allows them to coordinate their attacks to swarm the player if they arent careful. Due to all these changes, gunfights are far more intense in the original, and somehow make the perfect gameplay loop of the original even better. The story carries a more serious and sinister tone this time around, but Capcom did a great job adapting the story to this new tone while still maintaining the B-movie camp of the original title. Many of Leons classic one liners are maintained, while plenty of new ones are added. Some characters, such as Ashley and Luis, are given huge upgrades compared to their original counterparts. Many parts of the lore are fleshed out and expanded upon. The only downside with the writing is that certain characters, primarily Salazar and Saddler, are a downgrade compared to the original. Salazar in particular went from being one of the best characters in the original to forgettable in the remake. Overall though, the story is an upgrade from the original, even if I may prefer the more campy tone of the original. Overall, RE4 Remake is the perfect example of a modern remake done right. It maintains everything that made the original game special while adding on to it in ways that make sense. It sets a new standard for survival action horror games, and is a masterpiece in every single way. This is easily my favorite game from 2023. Final Rating: ILL BUY IT AT A HIGH PRICE 10/10
